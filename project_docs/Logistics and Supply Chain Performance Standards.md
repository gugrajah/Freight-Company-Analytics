**Transportation, Freight, and Carrier Performance**

* **On-Time Delivery (OTD) / On-Time In-Full (OTIF):** Measures the percentage of shipments delivered on time and complete. Shippers and carriers agree that the industry standard is 95% on-time delivery performance.  
* **On-Time Pick Up:** Measures whether carriers arrive for pickup at the agreed time. The industry standard is 95% on-time pick up.  
* **Appointment and Buffer KPIs:** The industry standard for what constitutes an "on-time" pickup or delivery is arriving within a **30-minute buffer** of the scheduled appointment. **Appointment scheduling compliance** is also heavily tracked.  
* **Primary Tender Acceptance Rate:** Tracks how often carriers accept loads at their contracted rates. The industry standard is an 85% acceptance rate.  
* **Cost Metrics:** These include total **Cost per shipment**, **Freight Cost per Unit**, **Cost per mile**, and **Cost per Ton-Kilometre** to evaluate true haulage efficiency.  
* **Accessorial Charges Breakdown:** Tracking extra fees generated by carriers, including detention time, layover charges, and redelivery fees.  
* **Claims and Damage Ratio:** The percentage of shipments featuring claims due to loss, damage, or delays.  
* **Transit Time Variance and Cycle Time:** Measures the difference between expected and actual transit times to identify bottlenecks, as well as the total journey time from the initial order to final delivery.  
* **Shipping Forecast Accuracy:** Comparing actual volume to customer forecasts, with the industry standard being within 10% of the forecasted volume.  
* **Lead Time:** The acceptable amount of notice given for truckload and LTL shipments. The industry standard is 2 days.  
* **Freight Tracking Compliance:** Ensuring carriers regularly provide shipment status updates.  
* **Payment Terms:** The industry standard for paying freight providers is within one week.

**Warehouse and Inventory Management**

* **Inbound/Outbound Volume:** Real-time tracking of receipts processed versus expectations, and orders picked, packed, and shipped versus targets.  
* **Pick Accuracy Rate:** The percentage of warehouse picks completed without errors. For example, accuracy below 99.5% often triggers a root-cause analysis.  
* **Dock-to-Stock Time:** The time elapsed from a trailer arriving at the dock to the inventory becoming available in the Warehouse Management System (WMS).  
* **Labor Productivity:** Measured in Units Per Labor Hour (UPLH) by function (receiving, putaway, picking, etc.) against engineered standards.  
* **Inventory Accuracy, Turnover, and Flow:** Cycle counts that measure the variance between system records and physical counts, as well as how quickly inventory moves through the supply chain.  
* **Space Utilization:** Heatmaps showing occupied versus available warehouse locations, which is critical for planning overflow.

**Fleet and Driver Management**

* **Fleet Uptime and Utilization:** Tracks how effectively transportation assets are deployed to prevent costly vehicle downtime.  
* **Cargo/Payload Utilization Rate:** Measures the percentage of a vehicle, container, or aircraft's capacity that is actually used per shipment.  
* **Route Optimization Scorecard:** Compares planned route miles against the actual miles driven to flag deviations.  
* **Driver Performance and Safety:** Tracks the number of miles moved per day, fuel consumption and efficiency, and **Safety and Incident Rates**, which monitor incidents per million kilometers, hard braking, speeding, and hours-of-service (HOS) violations.

**Port and Customs Operations**

* **Customs Clearance Time and Compliance Accuracy:** Measures the average time shipments take to clear customs and tracks documentation or paperwork errors.  
* **Marine Terminal KPIs:** Measures used to evaluate port operational efficiency include berth utilization, berth occupancy, ship turnaround time, and anchorage waiting time.

**Corporate Financial and Sustainability**

* **Financial Health:** Executive-level metrics such as Trading profit, EBITDA margin, Sales growth, and Inventory-to-sales ratios.  
* **Sustainability and Carbon Intensity:** Measuring emissions per load, renewable-fuel usage, solar PV contribution (percentage of electricity consumed), waste recycling targets (increasing cardboard and plastic recycling), and sustainable packaging (percentage of reusable, recyclable, compostable, or recycled content).
